Aerospace Engineer & Software Developer

Welcome to my page! I'm an engineer who just likes to build things. Rockets, rovers, drones, apps, websites. I have a wide range of interests and experience that brings surprising insight on the projects I'm on.

  • Website: You're here!
  • Email: chris.mcglownjr@tutanota.com
  • Location: Toledo, OH, USA
  • School: University of Cincinnati
  • Degree: Bachelors of Engineering
  • Freelance: Available

A quick background. I was born in Toledo, OH and graduated from St. John's Jesuit High School (that's what it was called then). The jesuit education instilled in me to be a Man for Others, to help and sacrifice for those less fortunate than you. Though I am not a devout Christian I still believe in the teachings of Christ. To accept others especially when they've fallen, cloth those with nothing on thier backs, and feed the hungry.

I went to the University of Cincinnati for my undergrad where I majored in Aerospace Engineering. As an undergrad I was involved in many projects doing research for my professors. With Dr. Bosse I was a part of the Autonomous Systems Lab where we designed a robot that onterfaced with a Mitsubishi robotic arm. This was used to study satelite capture techniques and remote brain surgery. For a short time I was a "Rocket Surgeon" lol. This is also when I started coding, not just for engineering projects but for research with the university. I would often get requests from professors who have an old Fortran code they want to revive.

The University of Cincinnati has a mandatory co-op system where you are required to complete a certain number of internships before you graduate. I did all my internships with the Air Force Research Laboratory at both Edwards AFB, CA and Wright-Patterson AFB, OH. My focus during this time was amassing expertise in Computational Fluid Dynamics and the regimes I studied was external flow, steady-state, internal combustion, and hypersonics. This is also the time I wrote a lot more software because sometimes you have to write your own solvers.

I'm now focusing on software development. Like most other engineers (besides the computer and electrical) I had to teach myself the programming languages I use. Python, C#, SQL, Bash, HTML, CSS, Fortran and a few others. I prefer to work in Python, C#, and SQL. I've built websites, converted excel toolkits to Python toolkits, written desktop applications in C#, and revived Fortran code for current computers. I study cloud technology (AWS) and data analysis. I'm always learning and improving.
