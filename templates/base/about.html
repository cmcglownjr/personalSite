I was born in Toledo, OH and graduated from University of Cincinnati where I majored in Aerospace Engineering. While at UC I was part of the Autonomous Systems Lab where we designed a robotic chassis that interfaced with a Mitsubishi industrial robotic arm used to study satellite capture techniques and remote brain surgery. I was a "Rocket Surgeon" for a short time. This is also when I started coding, not just for school projects but for research with the university as I would often get requests from professors who have an old Fortran code they want to revive.

The University of Cincinnati has a mandatory co-op system where you are required to complete a certain number of internships before you graduate. I did all my internships with the Air Force Research Laboratory at both Edwards AFB, CA and Wright-Patterson AFB, OH. My focus during this time was amassing expertise in Computational Fluid Dynamics and the regimes I studied was external flow, steady-state, internal combustion, and hypersonics. This is also the time I wrote a lot more software because sometimes you have to write your own solvers.

I'm now focusing on software development. I have taught myself Python, C#, SQL, Bash, HTML, CSS, and Fortran preferring to work in Python, C#, and SQL. I've built websites, converted excel toolkits to Python toolkits, written desktop applications in C#, and revived Fortran code for current computers. Currently, I'm studying cloud technology (AWS) and data analytics. I'm always learning and improving.
